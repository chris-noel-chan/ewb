Engineers Without Borders

Greetings, and welcome to our introduction site! You've probably got a lot of questions—and if you do, you're totally our kind of person—read on, friend!

Question:

Who are you?

Answer:

Engineers Without Borders (or EWB for short) is a charitable non-profit organization focused on the long-term development of third-world countries. We are a team of student and professional volunteers, overseas field operatives, and staff at our national office.

Contrary to what you might think, many of us are not in the field of engineering! We call ourselves engineers because our problem solving approach parallels that of trained engineers: systematic, innovative, and measurable. Why such an approach? Because we at EWB believe that in order to make a difference, it takes more than good intentions alone.

Question:

Who do you work for?

Answer:

We work for those who live in extreme poverty because of systemic neglect or injustice; those who have the fervent desires to make a better life for their communities, but face a lack of opportunity.

Though this scenario is present everywhere to some degree, EWB currently focuses efforts in a few select African countries where we've found the greatest need and the best investment of our efforts.

Question:

What do they need?

Answer:

Above all, we believe what the people we help need the most is the opportunity to help themselves. Our focus is not to provide foreign aid or relief, but to promote foreign development. Though we deeply respect and uphold the same values of those who aim to provide relief, we believe that systematic changes that work to build the capacity of a people is a more sustainable way out of extreme poverty.

Question:

How do you help them?

Answer:

We have a multitude of initiatives both overseas and at home. In Canada, we advocate for foreign policies and fair business practices that affect hard-working African entrepreneurs.

In Africa, our field operatives work with local government and community leaders on different projects to remove the various barriers between an African's hard work and a positive outcome.

We aim to create changes that last after we leave. We aim to work ourselves out of our job.

Thank you!

Thanks for taking the time to get to know us better! We hope that we've answered some of your basic questions about us…and intrigued you to ask more!

Before we say goodbye, feel free to browse through some of our resources, or get in touch with your local EWB network!
